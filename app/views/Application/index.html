Mode of Action

The therapeutic value of a chemical compound depends on what it does on the body rather than how it looks like. The Functional Therapeutic Chemical Classification System (FTC) abstracts away from the traditional chemical structure-based approach and focuses solely on the mode of action of drugs. The classification sorts therapeutic agents based on their function in the human body.

Drug Repurposing

A substance often does more than just one thing in the human body. Most of the times it results in side-effects to be prevented, but sometimes they can lead to valuable repositionning opportunities. The Functional Therapeutic Chemical Classification System (FTC) helps to discover and understand the implicit functions born by approved therapeutic compounds in the human system.

Open Source

From the web application code to drug repositionning hypotheses, everything is open and free. The Functional Therapeutic Chemical Classification System (FTC) integrates and leverages the public content of the Gene Ontology, DrugBank, Uniprot and GO annotations into a unique resource. Get started by looking at the documentation.
